Terms

Clear terms for using Textezi

Short product terms covering what Textezi does, when permissions are needed, how purchases work, and how support is handled.

Quick terms
Romanization only Textezi changes script, not meaning, tone, or translation.
Automation is optional In-place automation works only after the relevant Android permissions are enabled.
Trial and unlock The current access model is a 30-day trial with a one-time permanent unlock.
Rights reserved The app, site, branding, and public content remain protected intellectual property.
Product scope
Textezi is a romanization product. It preserves the language and changes only the script into Roman output for supported languages.
Launch support is intentionally narrower than the broader language list visible in Settings today.
Unsupported or not-yet-live languages may remain unchanged by design even if you can already select them in the app.
Permissions and feature prerequisites
Accessibility automation requires user consent and the relevant Android accessibility permission.
Suggest only mode and the floating bubble may also require display-over-other-apps permission.
Without the required permissions, Textezi may fall back to manual, share, or Process Text workflows instead of in-place automation.
Purchases and access
The app currently describes access as a soft 30-day trial followed by a one-time permanent unlock through Google Play.
Purchase availability, pricing labels, and billing readiness depend on Google Play configuration and the release state of the app.
Google Play purchases and account handling are also subject to Google Play’s own terms and policies.
Ownership and support
The app, website, branding, and published content are protected by copyright.
Support requests, bug reports, and feature suggestions currently run through the public Textezi issue tracker.
Public issue tracking does not change ownership of the product, brand, or source materials.
