Privacy

Private by default. Clear about boundaries.

Textezi is designed so the core converter works on-device, while optional automation stays limited to specific, user-approved situations.

Privacy at a glance
Core flow stays local Romanization, language choices, and saved corrections are designed to stay on the phone.
Sensitive fields are skipped Passwords, OTPs, phone inputs, and similar protected fields are intentionally excluded.
Preferences stay on-device Automation settings, disclosure acceptance, and floating bubble state are stored locally.
Google Play handles unlocks The one-time purchase flow depends on Google Play billing where it is enabled.
What stays local

The main Textezi experience runs on your phone

Supported text is romanized on-device for the core converter flow.
Selected languages, priority order, and preferred spellings stay local to the device.
Your saved corrections are part of the local quality loop, not a cloud-first requirement.
Where permissions matter

Automation is optional and bounded

Accessibility automation requires explicit Android permission and an in-app disclosure acceptance step.
Textezi is designed to act on committed text in editable fields you are actively using, not everywhere on the screen.
The floating bubble and minimized controls may also depend on display-over-other-apps permission.
Guardrails

Textezi only acts where it is meant to

The product direction is straightforward: keep the core converter local, avoid broad text handling, and stay away from fields that carry account or identity risk.

Textezi is not a custom keyboard and does not need to replace the keyboard you already use.
The core converter does not depend on a backend round-trip to produce Roman output.
Passwords, OTPs, login flows, phone fields, and similar protected inputs are intentionally left alone.
Billing and store-related purchase handling are managed through Google Play where the unlock flow is active.
